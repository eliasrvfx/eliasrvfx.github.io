Who I am

Technical Artist
on set & in engine

A Technical Artist working across film and real-time production — bridging physical sets and virtual worlds through on-set capture, VFX and Unreal Engine 5.

Some cinematic projects featured here use freely available community assets from libraries such as Fab, Quixel Megascans and the Unreal Marketplace. My focus in these projects is lighting, atmosphere, camera work, VFX and real-time pipeline — not modelling from scratch. This reflects how real productions work: using the best available resources efficiently to deliver results.

Breakdown 01

Dune Inspired

Recreating the vast, hostile desert environment of Arrakis inside Unreal Engine 5 — focusing on scale, atmosphere and the characteristic golden haze of the source material.

Built large-scale terrain using Landscape tools and Nanite-displaced meshes to achieve real sense of scale
Volumetric fog and Lumen sky atmosphere layered to create the iconic Arrakis dust haze
Camera animation in Sequencer — low, wide shots to emphasise the desert's scale
Custom sand shader with wind-driven surface detail and subsurface scattering on dune edges

Breakdown 02

Blade Runner Inspired

A neo-noir cityscape built in UE5 — capturing the cold, rain-soaked atmosphere of Blade Runner's dystopian Los Angeles through volumetric lighting, custom neon shaders and dynamic rain FX.

Modular city block system built in UE5 — dressed and stacked to create the layered urban density of the source
Custom emissive neon shader with bloom and lens flare — colour-graded to the blue/orange palette of the film
Rain FX system using Niagara — surface wetness material blended dynamically on geometry
Volumetric fog layers at multiple heights to create the layered smog atmosphere

Breakdown 03

Alien Us

Building dread through atmosphere — Alien Us was an exercise in restraint. Dark, claustrophobic environments where lighting and FX do the storytelling, not the geometry.

Tight, industrial corridor environments built with modular hard-surface kits — dressed with Megascans detail
Lighting pushed to near-black — single practical light sources with heavy Lumen indirect bounce for tension
Atmospheric particle systems — dust motes, steam leaks and condensation added through Niagara
Camera work deliberately slow — handheld feel simulated through Sequencer camera rigs

Breakdown 05

The Forest — PCG

A procedurally generated forest environment built entirely with UE5's PCG framework — exploring how to create dense, believable natural environments at scale without manual placement.

PCG graph built in UE5 to scatter and distribute foliage, rocks and ground detail procedurally across the terrain
Randomised FX system — atmospheric particles, mist and light shafts scattered non-destructively through the environment
Volumetric fog and Lumen lighting to create depth and mood through the forest canopy
Cinematic camera sequence through the environment — showcasing scale, atmosphere and real-time rendering quality

On-set capability

LIDAR & Photogrammetry — the full pipeline.

I've carried out LIDAR scanning and photogrammetry on real film productions — capturing sets, environments, vehicles, props and cast on location. That raw data is then processed and cleaned in RealityCapture, polygons optimised, and exported into Blender or Unreal Engine 5 — giving you production-ready assets and a fully navigable virtual set.

Step 01

On-Set Capture

LIDAR scanning and photogrammetry on location — sets, environments, vehicles, props and cast captured with production-grade precision.

LIDAR · Photogrammetry

Step 02

RealityCapture

Raw scan data processed in RealityCapture — point clouds meshed, cleaned, texture-baked and polygon count reduced for real-time use.

RealityCapture

Step 03

Blender / UE5

Optimised assets exported into Blender for any final cleanup, then brought into Unreal Engine 5 — lit, dressed and ready for real-time use.

Blender · Unreal Engine 5

Step 04

Virtual Scene

Scanned assets placed in UE5 — the real set recreated as a fully navigable virtual environment, lit and populated with scanned cast and props.

Virtual Production

Step 05

Previs & Review

Director and DOP explore every shot in real time — every lens, every camera move previsualized with real location data before principal photography.

Previs · Shot Review

This is a complete end-to-end pipeline I know from hands-on production experience — from arriving on set with scanning equipment, all the way through to a virtual production environment a DOP can explore shot by shot.
